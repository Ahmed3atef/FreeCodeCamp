Al-Khwarizmi

a Persian polymath who produced vastly influential Arabic-language works in mathematics, astronomy, and geography

Here's a time line of Al-Khwarizmi's life:

  • 780 - He was born in the city of Khwarizm
  • 813 - wrote a book called “Kitab al-Jabr wa al-Muqabala,” which introduces a new way of solving math problems using letters and symbols.
  • 820 - worked as a scholar in House of Wisdom, Baghdad
  • 830 - wrote another book called “Kitab Surat al-Ard” which talks about geography and how to make maps
  • 835 - translated both of his books into Latin. It helped people in Europe learn about his ideas and math concepts.
  • 850 - work on Indian numerals and the decimal system is introduced to Europe. This system, with numbers like 1, 2, and 3, becomes widely used and is still used today.
  • 850 - died in 850. Al-Khwarizmi’s work continues to influence scholars and mathematicians for centuries, and his legacy as pioneer of algebra and mathematics remains celebrated. People still study his work and learn from it.
